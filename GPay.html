Google Envelope: Smarter Money Management within Google Pay

1.0 My Story with Google Pay

As a student managing my pocket money through Google Pay, I struggled to organize funds for different purposes. I was inspired by a podcast where a scenario was discussed about the rapper’s saving habit: before buying anything, he’d save three times the item’s cost to ensure financial stability. Wanting to apply this method, I realized such feature is not available Google Pay to help with structured money management. This motivated me to design Google Envelope — a solution for organizing money into customizable sections, making financial management smarter and more intuitive.

2.0 Project Overview

Designing a money organization feature for Google Pay to help users manage their finances more effectively.

2.1 Problem

In Google Pay, users lacked an intuitive way to organize their money for different purposes, making it challenging to track spending across multiple needs. The absence of a built-in feature to categorize funds led to disorganized spending, reduced financial awareness, and difficulty managing budgets for specific goals.

2.2 Solution

The "Google Envelope" feature introduces customizable money sections within Google Pay, allowing users to create virtual envelopes for different spending categories. This empowers users to better allocate their money, track spending, and achieve financial goals with ease.

3.0 User Centered Design Process

To ensure the solution truly resonated with real user needs, I adopted a user-centered design process throughout the entire development of the Google Envelope case study — from research and ideation to prototyping and testing.

3.1 Brainstorm & Ideation

In managing personal finances, especially for students and young adults, there’s often a lack of accessible tools to organize money within digital payment apps. This gap makes it challenging to allocate funds for different purposes, track spending, and build better saving habits. Therefore, we aimed to design a solution for Google Pay that introduces a simple and intuitive envelope system, empowering users to manage their money more effectively and make mindful financial decisions.

Subsequently, I did user research based on real Users and surveys to define our main target users and features.
